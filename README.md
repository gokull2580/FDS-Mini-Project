Overview

Sales analysis and prediction rely on a mix of features (inputs/variables) and analytical techniques to understand past performance and forecast future sales. Here’s a clear breakdown of the most important features

Features
1. Historical Sales Data

Description:
Past sales records (daily, weekly, monthly).
Why it matters:
This is the foundation for any forecasting model—patterns like growth, decline, and seasonality come from here.

2. Time-Based Features

Description:
Date-related factors such as:

Day of week
Month
Quarter
Year
Holidays

Why it matters:
Helps capture seasonality and trends (e.g., higher sales during festivals or weekends).

3. Product Features

Description:
Details about the product:

Category
Price
Brand
Product lifecycle stage

Why it matters:
Different products behave differently—premium vs budget, new vs old items.

4. Pricing & Discounts

Description:

Selling price
Discounts/offers
Promotions

Why it matters:
Sales are highly sensitive to price changes. Discounts often create spikes.

5. Customer Features

Description:

Customer demographics (age, location)
Purchase history
Loyalty status

Why it matters:
Helps predict who will buy and how often.

6. Marketing & Advertising Data

Description:

Ad spend
Campaign type
Channel (online/offline)

Why it matters:
Marketing directly influences demand and sales volume.

7. External Factors

Description:

Economic conditions
Weather
Competitor actions

Why it matters:
These factors impact buying behavior beyond your control.

8. Inventory & Supply Chain

Description:

Stock levels
Lead time
Out-of-stock events

Why it matters:
You can’t sell what you don’t have—stockouts reduce sales artificially.

9. Store/Location Features

Description:

Store size
Location type (urban/rural)
Foot traffic

Why it matters:
Different locations have different demand patterns.

10. Lag Features (Important for ML)

Description:
Previous sales values used as predictors:

Sales yesterday
Sales last week
Moving averages

Why it matters:
Captures momentum and trends in data.
